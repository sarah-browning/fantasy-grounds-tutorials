Have you watched that famous group of "nerdy ass voice actors" that play D&D on Twitch every Thursday and thought it looked fun? Maybe you've been looking for new ways to engage with your friends and family in a socially distant manner? Just always wanted to give D&D a try but couldn't find local friends to play with? Then you're in the right place!

Dungeons & Dragons has seen a massive resurgence over the past few years due largely in part to the rise of streaming combined with the user friendliness of the 5th Edition ruleset released by Wizards of the Coast in 2014. Alongside this explosion of growth, a plethora of digital and online tools have emerged to aid in bringing tabletop games online and connecting players around the world. From dice rollers and character sheet generators to map-making tools, soundboards and token creators to fully-fledged virtual tabletop programs, it has never been easier to start playing D&D.

It is one of these virtual tabletop programs, Fantasy Grounds, that I'm going to introduce you to here. I've got a quick tutorial about how to create your first character in Fantasy Grounds when you're new to the program. Along the way, we'll learn some of the basics of using Fantasy Grounds as a player, including how to customize your dice. I want to also mention a few important things to note before we get started:

  • It is very important that the game master and all players are running the same version of Fantasy Grounds, and the easiest way to ensure this is to have everyone click on "Check for Updates" before each game session.
  • You'll need a copy of your character on the Gm's hosted server in order to play. To that end, this tutorial is going to assume that we are already connected to a server and creating a character for use in play.
  • We will also be using the official D&D theme, so it's possible that your Fantasy Grounds tabletop may look slightly different depending on the theme your GM has chosen. All the buttons should still be the same.
I'll be writing out instructions and including a short video for each step so you can see the process in action. So let's get started!

The first thing we need to do is activate the modules we'll be using to create our character. To do this, we'll click on Library and then Modules. The list of modules you see will vary depending on what your DM has made available to you. Click Load on any modules you want to use. You may need to wait a few seconds, but it'll be ready when the image of a book opens. We're just going to open the Player's Handbook. While we're here, we'll also select the "Create PC" layout from the Library tab to change our buttons to character creation relevant ones. This will help out in a moment.

The next thing we'll do is create a blank character sheet to start editing. If you click on the button for PCs in the right-hand bar, a new window will open up showing any character sheets you have access to. This is also how you will access your PC at the start of any game. For now, let's click on the Green + button to add a new item. A blank character sheet will open, and you'll notice that a blank square appears in the upper left corner of the screen. The first thing to do is enter a name for your character. You can do this later, but it makes it easy to find in the list of PCs in the event that you close out. Now that we have our scores in, we can begin creating the character in full. For this tutorial, we're going to create a sheet for Caelynn Kethaera, a Wood Elf Ranger. We're going to select the Outlander background, and give her a longbow and some starting equipment. As we go, you'll see the information on tabs such as Main, Skills, Abilities, Inventory, Notes, and Actions begin to fill out from Fantasy Grounds' automation.

Secondly, we're going to enter our character's ability scores to the sheet. In Fantasy Grounds, it's important to make sure we do this before adding other elements to the sheet so that bonuses from racials or character skills calculate properly. To add an ability score, simply click in the box for the appropriate score and type in the value. There are several different ways to calculate your ability scores. The most popular are using the standard array, point buy, or rolling four six-sided dice and dropping the lowest. We'll go ahead and use the standard array for this tutorial, which is 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, and 16. For Caelynn, we'll assign the 8 to her STR, 16 to DEX, 14 to CON, 12 to INT, 15 to WIS, and 10 to CHA. As you enter your scores, you may notice the numbers for your AC, Init and Saves changing. This is normal.

One of the reasons for Fantasy Grounds' popularity is because of the automation it offers, and character creation is no different. Much of the chracter creation is handled through drag and dropping elements onto the character sheet, and the program then fills out what is needed. Fantasy Grounds is a really robust program, and if you're not sure what you want your character to be, you may want to spend some time looking and reading through the options. The modules include all of the same information that is in the official source books, it just also includes a bunch of automation to work within the program and make things easier. For character creation, I like to start with Race followed by Background and finally, Class. You can access these sections by clicking on the appropriate buttons in the right-hand bar, and then selecting hte option you're interested in reading more about. Once you've made a selection, simply click on the red dragon icon (sometimes a shield icon in other themes) beside the selection, and dragging that to the appropriate section of the character sheet. If the selection you made requires a sub-selection, a small window will autoamtically pop up giving you options to choose from. In the below video, you'll see this happen for a subrace when we choose our race, and again for skills when we select our class.

The next thing to do is add some starting equipment, including a weapon, armor and backpack kit. You can add these from the Items button on the right-hand bar. If you know what you're looking for, you can simply search it, or you can scroll through the lists until you see something you like. We'll add a longbow, some studed leather armor, and an explorer's pack. In the case of items, we'll need to switch tabs on the character sheet to the Inventory tab and drop our items there. In the case of weapons, they will automatically add an action to the Actions tab on the character sheet, so you can later roll your attack and damage rolls. If you drop a pack, such as our Explorer's Pack on the Inventory page, it will add everything that in included in that pack automatically, saving heaps of time by not requiring you to find each individual item included.

We're nearly done. The very last thing to do is select a character portrait to represent your character in the game. We do this by clicking on the blank icon near our name. This will bring up a window with a selection of bags. Hovering over each one will tell you what collection they include, and you can double click to delve deeper in and see the options. When you find one that you like, simply double click and it will show up in the upper left portion of your character sheet, as well as in the upper left section of the screen. You can also import your own portraits, but we'll cover how to do that in another tutorial. For our purposes today, we'll select one of the already installed portraits.

And that's your first character created in Fantasy Grounds! Congratulations. I know the program can feel overwhelming at first, but it's really not so bad once you get used to it. As a bonus, I'll also show you how to customize the color of your dice so you can stand out from the rest of your party.

Everyone likes to have their own dice, and you can customize the color of yours so they show up in the chat differently from everyone elses. To do so, you're going to click on the Colors button, which shows up as an artist's palette in the mini-buttons at the top of the right-hand bar. A new window will open showing your current dice color in the middle, surrounded by various sized circles of red, blue, green, white, and black. Clicking on these colored circles will add either a lot, some, or a little of that color to the dice color. Play around until you like the color in the center and then click the X to close the window. There is also a circle marked with a T, which will switch the text on your dice to either white or black. As you make changes, you'll see your dice change in real time. Adding white or black can brighten or darken the color as you wish.
